# banner_prompting.md

# Purpose

Prompt patterns for generating banner design concept directions with image2 workflows.

Use these prompts for concept exploration, not final artwork production.

# Core Layers

Every banner prompt should include:

1. subject
2. style
3. composition
4. variation directives
5. production notes

# Banner-Specific Priorities

Prioritize:

- headline readability
- offer clarity
- strong visual hierarchy
- quick recognition
- commercial impact

Avoid:

- too much small text
- decorative detail that reduces readability
- final production instructions

# Prompt Pattern

```text
Subject:
Create a concept direction for a [banner type] promoting [event/product/service].
Main message: "[headline]".
Required supporting text: [supporting copy].
Audience: [target audience].

Style:
Use a [mood] commercial banner style with [typography direction].
Color direction: [palette or contrast direction].
The design should feel [tone] and suitable for [placement/context].

Composition:
Make the headline the primary focal point.
Use [layout structure] to separate headline, offer, and supporting text.
Place graphics or product imagery in [placement].
Keep readability strong from [viewing distance].

Variation directives:
Generate three concept directions.
A: typography-led banner with maximum headline impact.
B: image-led banner with strong product or event recognition.
C: minimal high-contrast banner optimized for distance readability.

Production notes:
Treat this as a starting concept only.
Prioritize typography hierarchy, composition, visual impact, and commercial readability.
Keep the concept easy to refine in Illustrator.
```

# Example

```text
Subject:
Create a concept direction for a horizontal event banner promoting a local summer night market.
Main message: "Summer Night Market".
Required supporting text: Food, Music, Handmade Goods, July 12-14.
Audience: local families and young adults.

Style:
Use a lively but clean commercial event style with bold friendly headline typography.
Color direction: warm yellow, deep navy, white, and small red accents.
The design should feel energetic, easy to read, and suitable for street display.

Composition:
Make "Summer Night Market" the primary focal point.
Use a centered headline structure with supporting details grouped below.
Place simple food and music graphics around the headline without crowding it.
Keep readability strong from 10-20 meters.

Variation directives:
Generate three concept directions.
A: typography-led banner with maximum headline impact.
B: illustration-led banner with simple food and music icons.
C: minimal high-contrast banner optimized for distance readability.

Production notes:
Treat this as a starting concept only.
Prioritize typography hierarchy, composition, visual impact, and commercial readability.
Keep the concept easy to refine in Illustrator.
```
